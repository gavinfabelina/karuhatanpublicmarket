Privacy Policy

1. Background

Republic Act No. 10173, also known as the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (DPA), aims to protect personal data in information and communications systems both in the government and the private sector.

It ensures that entities or organizations processing personal data establish policies, and implement measures and procedures that guarantee the safety and security of personal data under their control or custody, thereby upholding an individual’s data privacy rights. A personal information controller or personal information processor is instructed to implement reasonable and appropriate measures to protect personal data against natural dangers such as accidental loss or destruction, and human dangers such as unlawful access, fraudulent misuse, unlawful destruction, alteration, and contamination.

To inform its personnel of such measures, each personal information controller or personal information processor is expected to produce a Privacy Manual. The Manual serves as a guide or handbook for ensuring the compliance of an organization or entity with the DPA, its Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR), and other relevant issuances of the National Privacy Commission (NPC). It also encapsulates the privacy and data protection protocols that need to be observed and carried out within the organization for specific circumstances (e.g., from collection to destruction), directed toward the fulfillment and realization of the rights of data subjects.

2. Introduction

The Karuhatan Public Market hereby adopts this Privacy Manual to comply with the Republic Act No. 10173 or Data Privacy Act of 2012 (DPA), its Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR), and other relevant policies, including issuances of the National Privacy Commission. The Karuhatan Public Market respects and values your data privacy rights, and makes sure that all personal data collected from you, the owners, collectors, tenants, and prospective tenants, are processed in adherence to the general principles of transparency, legitimate purpose, and proportionality.

This Manual serves as a guide to inform you of the Karuhatan Public Market’s data protection and security measures, which will help you in exercising your rights under the DPA.

3. Definitions

CONSENT refers to any freely given, specific, informed indication of will, whereby the Data Subject agrees to the collection and processing of his Personal, Sensitive Personal, or Privileged Information. Consent shall be evidenced by written, electronic, or recorded means. It may also be given on behalf of a Data Subject by a lawful representative or an agent specifically authorized by the Data Subject to do so.

DATA PROTECTION OFFICER refers to an individual appointed by the Group or Organization, who shall have the qualifications, and who shall perform the functions, of a data protection officer as provided in this Manual.

DATA SUBJECT refers to an individual whose personal, sensitive personal or privileged information is processed by the organization.

KARUHATAN PUBLIC MARKET referred to as the “organization”, “company”, “we”, “our”, or “us”.

PERSONAL DATA BREACH refers to a breach of security leading to accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to, Personal Data transmitted, stored, or otherwise processed.

PERSONAL INFORMATION refers to any information whether recorded in a material form or not, from which the identity of an individual is apparent or can be reasonably and directly ascertained by the entity holding the information, or when put together with other information would directly and certainly identify an individual.

PERSONAL INFORMATION CONTROLLER refers to a natural or juridical person, or any other body who controls the processing of personal data, or instructs another to process personal data on his behalf.

PERSONAL INFORMATION PROCESSOR refers to any natural or juridical person or any other body to whom a personal information controller may outsource or instruct the processing of personal data pertaining to a data subject.

PRIVACY IMPACT ASSESSMENT is a process undertaken and used to evaluate and manage the impact on privacy of a particular project, program, process, or measure.

PROCESSING refers to any operation or any set of operations performed upon personal information including, but not limited to, the collection, recording, organization, storage, updating or modification, retrieval, consultation, use, consolidation, blocking, erasure or destruction of data.

SECURITY INCIDENT is an event or occurrence that affects or tends to affect data protection, or may compromise the availability, integrity, and confidentiality of Personal Data. It includes incidents that would result in a Personal Data Breach.

5. Processing of Personal Data

The various data phases (or processing systems) used by Karuhatan Public Market are described in this section. These include the gathering of personal data, its actual use, storage or retention, and destruction.

Collection (e.g. type of data collected, mode of collection, person collecting information, etc.)

  • Karuhatan Public Market obtains the necessary personal details, such as the prospective tenant, approved tenant, and existing tenant's complete name, address, email address, and phone number, in addition to a valid ID and a cedula. Through the completion of the tenancy application and to the approved and existing tenants, the market owner handling this will get such information.

Use

  • The Karuhatan Public Market will utilize the collected personal data for documentation needs, generating tenancy agreements, rent payment tracking, and financial reports within the system.
  • Providing tenants with access to their rental payment history, including details on delayed payment fees and advance payment coverage, through their user dashboard.
  • Facilitating communication between market management (market owner and rent collector) and tenants regarding lease agreements, rental payments, and other market-related matters.

Storage, Retention, and Destruction (e.g. means of storage, security measures, form of information stored, retention period, disposal procedure, etc.)

  • Karuhatan Public Market shall make sure that any personal information in its possession remains protected from unauthorized or unintentional disclosure, alteration, and destruction, as well as from any other unauthorized processing.
  • Storing tenant information securely within the web-based system to ensure its confidentiality and integrity, will store gathered personal information using the proper security measures.
  • Implementing appropriate security measures to protect stored personal data from unauthorized access, alteration, or disclosure.
  • Setting retention periods for tenant data, ensuring that data is not retained longer than necessary for operational purposes.
  • Establishing procedures for the secure destruction of tenant data once it is no longer needed or when requested by tenants.

Access (e.g. personnel authorized to access personal data, purpose of access, mode of access, request for amendment of personal data, etc.)

  • Given the risky and private nature of the personal information in the company's possession, limiting access to tenant data to authorized personnel only, such as market owners, rent collectors, and designated administrators.
  • Implementing authentication mechanisms to verify the identity of users accessing tenant data within the system.
  • Providing tenants with the ability to access and update their own personal information within the system, subject to verification and approval processes.

Disclosure and Sharing (e.g. individuals to whom personal data is shared, disclosure of policy and processes, outsourcing and subcontracting, etc.)

  • Restricting the disclosure of tenant information to third parties unless required by law or with explicit consent from the tenants.
  • Implementing confidentiality agreements and data protection measures when sharing tenant data with authorized recipients, such as government agencies or legal authorities.
  • Applicant and tenant information may be disclosed in business transactions such as mergers or acquisitions as part of due diligence processes, subject to confidentiality agreements and applicable laws.
  • The Karuhatan Public Market may disclose applicant and tenant information to government authorities in compliance with legal requirements for investigations or audits.

6. Security Measures

As a personal information controller or personal information processor, an organization must implement reasonable and appropriate physical, technical and organizational measures for the protection of personal data. Security measures aim to maintain the availability, integrity and confidentiality of personal data and protect them against natural dangers such as accidental loss or destruction, and human dangers such as unlawful access, fraudulent misuse, unlawful destruction, alteration and contamination. In this section, you give a general description of those measures.

Organization Security Measures

Every personal information controller and personal information processor must also consider the human aspect of data protection. The provisions under this section shall include the following:

  • Data Protection Officer (DPO), or Compliance Officer for Privacy (COP) - The designated Data Protection Officer is Mr. Andy Lopez, who is concurrently serving as the Market Owner of the Karuhatan Public Market.
  • Functions of the DPO, COP and/or any other responsible personnel with similar functions - The Data Protection Officer (DPO) shall ensure the organization's adherence to the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (DPA), its Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR), and relevant policies. The DPO oversees compliance with these regulations, including the conduction of Privacy Impact Assessments (PIAs), implementation of appropriate security measures, establishment of protocols for addressing security incidents and data breaches, and management of inquiries and complaints procedures. The DPO's responsibilities encompass fostering a culture of privacy and data protection within the organization, ensuring that the web-based tenant management system operates in alignment with legal requirements, particularly regarding data handling, security, incident response, and complaint resolution as mandated by the DPA and its associated regulations.
  • Conduct of trainings or seminars to keep personnel, especially the Data Protection Officer updated vis-à-vis developments in data privacy and security - To ensure that everyone, especially the Data Protection Officer (DPO), stays up-to-date with changes in data privacy and security, the organization shall organize regular training sessions or seminars. It would be mandatory for all staff to attend training on data privacy and security at least once a year. For those directly handling personal data, we'll make sure they participate in relevant training sessions as often as needed to keep their knowledge current and ensure compliance with regulations and best practices.
  • Conduct of Privacy Impact Assessment (PIA) - The organization will carry out Privacy Impact Assessments (PIAs) for all activities, projects, and systems that involve processing personal data. We have the option to hire a third party to conduct the PIA if needed.
  • Recording and documentation of activities carried out by the DPO, or the organization itself, to ensure compliance with the DPA, its IRR and other relevant policies. - The Data Protection Officer (DPO) will record and document all activities undertaken. This includes the organization's commitment to conducting yearly trainings on data privacy and security, which will be documented as part of the ongoing efforts to uphold regulatory standards and enhance staff awareness and capabilities in handling personal data responsibly. This documentation serves as a comprehensive record of efforts made towards adherence to regulatory requirements, facilitating transparency and accountability within the organization's compliance framework.
  • Duty of Confidentiality - The organization shall maintain confidentiality by ensuring that only authorized personnel can access sensitive information. It will use security measures like encryption and restricted access to protect data. Regular training will ensure that the staff understand the importance of confidentiality. We also have agreements in place to formalize commitments to confidentiality. If there are breaches, the organization shall investigate and take appropriate action.
  • Review of Privacy Manual - The Privacy Manual would undergo an annual review and evaluation process to ensure its effectiveness and relevance to the Karuhatan Public Market. Privacy and security policies and practices within the organization would be updated regularly to align with current data privacy best practices and evolving regulatory requirements. This ongoing review ensures that our approach to privacy remains robust and adaptable to changing circumstances, enabling us to maintain the highest standards of data protection and compliance.

Physical Security Measures

This portion shall feature the procedures intended to monitor and limit access to the facility containing the personal data, including the activities therein. It shall provide for the actual design of the facility, the physical arrangement of equipment and furniture, the permissible modes of transfer, and the schedule and means of retention and disposal of data, among others. To ensure that mechanical destruction, tampering and alteration of personal data under the custody of the organization are protected from man-made disasters, power disturbances, external access, and other similar threats, provisions like the following must be included in the Manual:

  • Format of data to be collected - Personal data collected for the tenant management system may be in digital/electronic or paper-based/physical formats, providing flexibility in storage and processing.
  • Storage type and location (e.g. filing cabinets, electronic storage system, personal data room/separate room or part of an existing room) - All personal data processed for the tenant management system is securely stored in a designated data room. Paper documents are stored in locked filing cabinets, while digital files are stored on company-provided computers.
  • Access procedure of agency personnel - Entry into the data room is restricted to authorized personnel. Each authorized individual receives a duplicate key for access, with additional access granted by the Data Protection Officer upon request approval.
  • Monitoring and limitation of access to room or facility - Access to the data room is closely monitored and controlled. Authorized personnel must register their entry through the organization's online platform and log their access details in a physical logbook at the room entrance.
  • Design of office space/work station - Office spaces and workstations are configured to ensure privacy and protect the processing of personal data. Computers are spaced apart to maintain confidentiality during data processing.
  • Persons involved in processing, and their duties and responsibilities - Individuals involved in processing personal data for the tenant management system are required to maintain confidentiality and integrity. They are prohibited from bringing personal gadgets or storage devices into the data storage room to prevent unauthorized access.
  • Modes of transfer of personal data within the organization, or to third parties - Personal data transfers within the organization or to third parties use secure methods, such as encrypted emails, to maintain data integrity and confidentiality.
  • Retention and disposal procedure - Personal data is retained for a specified period, such as 2 to 3 contract years. Upon expiration, all physical and electronic copies of personal data are securely destroyed and disposed of using approved methods to prevent unauthorized access or retrieval.

Technical Security Measures

Each personal information controller and personal information processor must implement technical security measures to make sure that there are appropriate and sufficient safeguards to secure the processing of personal data, particularly the computer network in place, including encryption and authentication processes that control and limit access. They include the following, among others:

  • Monitoring for security breaches - The organization employs an intrusion detection system to continuously monitor for security breaches and promptly alerts relevant personnel of any attempts to disrupt or compromise the system's integrity.
  • Security features of the software/s and application/s used - Before the usage of the system, all applications must undergo thorough review and evaluation to ensure compatibility with existing security protocols and operational requirements specific to the Karuhatan Public Market.
  • Process for regularly testing, assessment and evaluation of effectiveness of security measures - A structured process is established for conducting regular testing, assessment, and evaluation of security measures. This includes reviewing security policies, performing vulnerability assessments, and conducting penetration testing as prescribed by the appropriate department or unit.
  • Encryption, authentication process, and other technical security measures that control and limit access to personal data - Access to personal data within the tenant management system is controlled and restricted through encryption, authentication processes, and other technical security measures. Personnel accessing personal data must verify their identity using secure encrypted links and undergo multi-level authentication procedures to prevent unauthorized access.

7. Breach and Security Incidents

Every personal information controller or personal information processor must develop and implement policies and procedures for the management of a personal data breach, including security incidents. This section must adequately describe or outline such policies and procedures, including the following:

  • Creation of a Data Breach Response Team - A Data Breach Response Team comprising of four (4) designated officers shall be established to ensure immediate action in the event of a security incident or personal data breach within the Karuhatan Public Market, one for each phase. The team shall conduct an initial assessment of the incident or breach to ascertain its nature and extent. They are tasked with executing measures to mitigate the adverse effects of the incident or breach promptly.
  • Measures to prevent and minimize occurrence of breach and security incidents - The organization shall conduct Privacy Impact Assessments once a month specific for the Karuhatan Public Market to identify risks in the processing system and to monitor for security breaches and vulnerabilities in computer networks. Personnel directly involved in the processing of personal data within the system must attend trainings and seminars for capacity building. Additionally, there must be periodic reviews of policies and procedures implemented within the organization to ensure their effectiveness and relevance.
  • Procedure for recovery and restoration of personal data - The organization shall maintain a comprehensive backup system for all personal data stored within the Tenant Management System for the Karuhatan Public Market. In the event of a security incident or data breach, the team shall compare the backup with the affected file to identify any inconsistencies or alterations resulting from the incident or breach. This process ensures the integrity and accuracy of the restored data.
  • Notification protocol - The Head of the Data Breach Response Team, which is the DPO, shall be responsible for informing the management of the need to notify the National Privacy Commission (NPC) and the data subjects affected by the incident or breach within the period prescribed by law. Management may delegate the actual notification process to the head of the Data Breach Response Team, ensuring timely and compliant communication with relevant parties.
  • Documentation and reporting procedure of security incidents or a personal data breach - The Data Breach Response Team shall maintain detailed documentation of every security incident or personal data breach encountered within the Tenant Management System for the Karuhatan Public Market. Additionally, an annual report summarizing all incidents and breaches shall be prepared by the team and submitted to management and the NPC within the prescribed period, ensuring transparency and accountability in handling such incidents.
